### What makes Focus Bear different from Freedom, Cold Turkey, Tiimo, Routinery or other such apps?
Freedom and Cold Turkey only block distractions, while Tiimo and Routinery only offer routine management. Focus Bear offers both distraction blocking and routine management, as well as a bunch of other features such as pomodoro-like focus session timer, motivations, and a brain dump.

### If you were a user, why would you choose Focus Bear over competitors?
Not only does Focus Bear combine features from other apps into one package so it's a lot more convenient, it's also built with neurodivergent people in mind. Most other apps like Focus Bear don't take that into account. If I had autism, ADHD, or any other disorder, that would be another point for me to use Focus Bear. 

### What's one feature that other apps have that Focus Bear doesn't?
Tiimo, for instance, has an AI-powered visual planner that takes in to-dos and visualizes them. I feel like Focus Bear would benefit a lot from a feature like that, where your routines are laid out in a more visual manner instead of just being in a list.

### Based on your research, what's one improvement you think Focus Bear could make?
Visualize routines and habits, either automatically through AI or manually through the user, like having a color-coded calendar, or a round clock-styled visualizer for daily routines. Also just general improvements to the UI/UX to make it more intuitive and less cluttered, especially the Windows app.

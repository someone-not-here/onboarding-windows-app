## Research & Learn

### How does a Kanban board work, and how does it help manage workflow?
A Kanban board is a visual workflow management tool that uses columns to represent stages of a process and cards to represent individual tasks or items. Cards are moved from left to right across the columns as work progresses, and the number of cards allowed in a single column is limited to prevent bottlenecks. It helps manage workflow by visualizing the status of various tasks, helping teams highlight issues and identify inefficiencies so they can optimize their workflow.

### What do the different columns on a Kanban board represent?
- **Backlog**: Tasks that are planned but not yet started.
- **Ready**: Tasks ready to be picked up.
- **In Progress**: Tasks that are currently being worked on.
- **In Review**: Tasks that have been completed but are awaiting review, testing, or approval.
- **Done / Approved**: Tasks that have been completed and approved.
- **Not Approved**: Tasks that failed to pass review and need fixing.

### How do tasks move through the board, and who is responsible for updating them?
Tasks move across the board, from left to right, as work progresses. Movement happens continuously as soon as a task is ready for the next stage, guided by work-in-progress limits to avoid bottlenecks. Team members themselves are responsible for updating the board, typically during daily stand-ups or as soon as a status changes.

### What are the benefits of limiting work in progress (WIP)?
- **Reduces multitasking and switching**: Teams focus on fewer tasks at once, leading to faster completion and higher quality work.
- **Helps identify bottlenecks**: Limits help visualize overloads and blockers, allowing the team to address issues before they pile up.
- **Improves workflow flow**: Encourages smoother, continuous movement of tasks.
- **Increases predictability and throughput**: Stable cycle times help forecast delivery and boost overall productivity.
- **Reduces stress**: Less stress leads to fewer errors and better team morale.


## Reflection

### How does Kanban help manage priorities and avoid overload?
Kanban allows teams to visually order tasks based on urgency, value, or deadlines. It avoids overload through work-in-progress limits on each column, which prevent starting too many tasks at once and force the team to finish existing work before pulling new ones.

### How can you improve your workflow using Kanban principles?
I can improve my workflow by using the Kanban board to visualize tasks already in progress. I can then focus on finishing those tasks first, instead starting too many tasks all at once and overwhelming myself.

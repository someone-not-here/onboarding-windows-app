## Research & Learn

### What is Agile, and how does it differ from traditional project management?
Agile is an iterative and flexible approach to software development that focuses on delivering small, continuous improvements instead of large releases at the end of a project. Agile adapts to change and encourages frequent feedback from stakeholders.

Agile is a flexible and iterative approach to software development that emphasizes delivering continuous improvements in short development cycles (sprints), as opposed to culminating improvements and changes into large releases at the end of a project like some traditional project management approaches. Agile also gives room for any change in requirements and response to feedback, even late into development.

### What are the core values and principles of Agile?
The core values of Agile are:
- Individuals and interactions over processes and tools
- Working software over comprehensive documentation
- Customer collaboration over contract negotiation
- Responding to change over following a plan

The 12 core principles are:
- Highest priority is satisfying the customer
- Welcome changing requirements, even late into development
- Delvier working software frequently
- Business people and developers must work together
- Build projects around motivated individuals
- Promote face-to-face conversations
- Working software is the primary measure of progress
- Agile processes promote sustainable development
- Continuous attention to technical excellence and good design enhances agility
- Simplicity — the art of maximizing the amount of work not done — is essential
- The best architectures, requirements, and designs emerge from self-organizing teams
- At regular intervals, the team reflects on how to become more effective

### What are the differences between Scrum and Kanban, and when is each used?
Scrum is a structured and iterative framework with fixed sprint durations, defined roles, and ceremonies such as sprint planning, daily stand-ups, reviews, and retrospectives. It's best used when projects have changing requirements but benefit from regular feedback cycles, clear deadlines, and cross-functional teams.

Kanban is a continuous flow system with no fixed iterations or prescribed roles. It uses a "Kanban board" to limit work-in-progress, visualize workflow, and focus on continuous delivery and optimizing flow by identifying bottlenecks. It's best used for teams with variable workloads where steady flow and efficiency matter most.

### Why does Focus Bear lean towards Kanban instead of Scrum?
Kanban allows for more flexibility and continuous delivery. Work can be prioritized and adjusted at any time — there's no need to wait for the end of a sprint. This fits well with the asynchronous nature of development.

## Reflection

### What do you think are the biggest benefits and challenges of Agile?

Agile's biggest benefits are flexibility, faster feedback cycles, better collaboration between teams, and providing the ability to adapt to changing requirements. 

A challenge that could come up with Agile is having to manage frequent changes without losing focus. Another challenge is maintaining alignments across various teams, especially when development is asynchronous and requirements and priority shift often.

### How can Agile principles help improve your role and responsibilities?
They help me focus on delivering improvements to the project in increments as opposed to completing everything at once. Because of its incremental nature, it also allows me to take in feedback continuously without having to wait to finish a module first.

## Research & Learn

### What are the key roles in an Agile team, and what does each role do?
- Developer
    - The "main body" of the team, they develop and build features and maintain the code base through bug fixes and updates.
- QA
    - They help ensure the product meets a standard of quality before release. They hunt bugs and test features.
- Product Manager
    - They help define the vision of product, prioritizng features to ensure that it aligns with the business and end user needs.
- Agile Project Manager
    - They manage and oversee the Agile team. They organize ceremonies, help deal with blockers, and helps the team to stay on track.
- Stakeholders
    - They provide feedback, issue requirements, and come up with business goals that determine the overall direction of the product.

### How do these roles collaborate in Kanban vs. Scrum?
- In Scrum:
    - Developers and QA: They work together inside fixed-length sprints. They pull from a Sprint Backlog, do daily stand-ups, and aim to deliver a potentially shippable increment by sprint end.
    - Product Manager: Owns and prioritizes the product backlog, clarifies requirements, and accepts/rejects work at sprint review.
    - Agile Team Manager (Scrum Master): Facilitates the process, removes impediments, coaches the team on Scrum, and runs ceremonies.
- In Kanban:
    - Developers and QA: They work together on a shared visual board with columns. They pull new work when capacity allows. QA often works alongside devs in a flow-based way.
    - Product Manager: Continuously prioritizes the backlog. Replenishes the board as needed with no formal sprint planning.
    - Agile Team Manager (Kanban Coach/Flow Manager): Focuses on flow optimization, metrics, removing blockers, and improving the process.

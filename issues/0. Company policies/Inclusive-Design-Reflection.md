## Research

### Who are considered vulnerable populations, and what challenges might they face in digital spaces?

Those considered vulnerable populations are:
- People with ADHD, autism, or other neurodevelopmental conditions.
- People with cognitive or sensory disabilities.
- People facing challenges in mental health.

The challenges they might face in digital spaces include:
- Overstimulation and/or confusion due to overwhelming/noisy UIs.
- Feeling pressured by traditional productivity tools.

### What ethical considerations are important when designing or working with neurodivergent individuals?
- Use supportive and forgiving language; avoid messaging based on guilt or time pressure.
- Allow customizability for the end user so they may tweak the app/program to fit their needs.
- Be transparent, especially when handling user data.


### How can you make interactions and content more accessible for people with ADHD or Autism?
- Use a simple, readable, and non-overstimulating UI. Offer calm and soothing color schemes. Don't clutter the UI with unneccessary animations or pop-ups.
- Ensure the language used is kind, respectful, and constructive.

### How can we support neurodivergent team members in a professional setting?
- Avoid using a sarcastic or condescending tone when speaking with them. When speaking through text, be sure to be clear and careful in your wording.
- Be patient with them. Don't rush them unnecessarily.
- Respect their different schedules, workflows, and ways of doing things.
- Offer help when they need it.

## Reflection

### How can you adjust your communication style to be more inclusive of neurodivergent users and teammates?

I can:
- Avoid using vague language. Be clear in my wording.
- Adopt a friendly tone without sounding condescending.
- Avoid rushing unless absolutely necessary.
- Offer reassurance, validation, and praise whenever it's called for.

### What are some common UX or communication pitfalls that might make Focus Bear less accessible or supportive?
- An overwhelming, cluttered UI.
- Lack of customizable settings so that end users can tweak the app/program to suit their needs.
- A lack of clarity in instructions or text.
- Aggressive, nagging notifications.

### What is one practical change you can make in your work to better support vulnerable populations?
Keep things simple: advocate for a simple UX, and allow extensive customizability. Users won't get overwhelmed right from the start, and they can tweak the app to suit their individual needs.

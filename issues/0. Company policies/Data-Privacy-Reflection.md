### What types of data are considered confidential at Focus Bear?
- Contact information (phone number, emails, etc.)
- Data on personal habits
- Device information
- Financial information for paying users

### What are the best practices for handling confidential data?
- Have clear policies and incident responses. Define confidentiality policies, data breach response plans, and conduct regular audits/compliance checks.
- Use secure storage and tools. Avoid storing data in personal devices, unsecured cloud services, or email attachments.
- Encrypt the data, both stored and data in transit. Use strong encryption methods.

### How should you respond to a suspected data breach or accidental disclosure of confidential information?
- Report a suspected data breach immediately to the people responsible for handling such events. 
- Notify others in my area of the data breach. 
- Take immediate steps in further securing the data I handle.

## Reflection
### What steps can you take to ensure you handle data securely in your daily tasks?
- Keep the software I use up-to-date, especially when it comes to security updates.
- Avoid sharing data unnecessarily.
- Use data encryption.

### How should you store, share, and dispose of sensitive information safely?
- Storing: Use strong passwords, minimize copies, classify data properly.
- Sharing: Use principle of least privilege, avoid unsecure methods of transfer, encrypt files before sharing.
- Disposal: use secure deletion methods (overwrite data, factory reset), wipe cloud accounts properly, avoid premature deletion of data.

### What are some common mistakes that lead to data privacy issues, and how can they be avoided?
- Storing data in plain text. Avoid this by encrypting the data.
- Sharing data with third parties without proper consent. Avoid this by using Data Processing Agreements. Obtain explicit consent where required, and conduct vendor due diligence/privacy impact assessments.
- Weak or reused passwords and poor access controls. Avoid this by enforcing strong passwords and multi-factor authentication, and using role-based access control and the principle of least privilege.

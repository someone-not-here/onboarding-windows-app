## Research

### What AI tools are typically used for your role?
- Coding assistants are typically used — tools like ChatGPT, Grok, or Claude can be used to generate code based on prompts, or to go over already written code and point out mistakes or potential bugs.

### What are the benefits and risks of using AI in a professional setting?
- Benefits include:
    - Saving time/faster output
    - A boost to productivity
    - Easier maintainability

- Risks include:
    - Potential copyright infringement and other legal risks
    - Incorrect output
    - Complacency or laziness within the programmer

### What types of information should never be entered into AI tools?
- Login information like passwords
- Sensitive, work-related information such as user data
- Personal information such as phone numbers, emails, medical records, etc.

### How can you fact-check and validate AI-generated content to ensure accuracy?
Cross-reference the content against primary sources (official websites, peer-reviewed papers, etc.), use tools like search engines, academic archives, or direct verification from original authors, and always apply critical thinking (check consistency, biases, and recency).

## Reflection
### When should you use AI for assistance, and when should you rely on your own skills?
You should only use AI when you are either completely stumped on a problem or to create something that's small, will only be used temporarily or sparingly, and can be created faster with AI (like a small .bat file that automates a task). If you have an idea for a solution in your head, you should use your own skills instead.

### How can you avoid over-reliance on AI while still benefiting from it?
Treat AI as an assistant and not as a replacement for yourself. You should be doing the bulk of the work, with AI being there to help with the smaller tasks and any truly difficult problems wherein you are stumped.

### What steps will you take to ensure data privacy when using AI tools?
- Only use trusted AI tools.
- Don't input any sensitive or personal information into any of these tools.

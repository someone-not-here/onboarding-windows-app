## Research

### What are common cyber security threats in a remote work environment?
- Phishing
- Unsecure home or public Wi-Fi networks
- Social engineering
- Malware/ransomware attacks
- Vulnerabilities in personal devices


### What are best practices for keeping your devices and accounts secure?
- Enable multi-factor authentication, like 2FA.
- Use strong passwords for every account and website.
- Avoid using public networks when doing work-related tasks.
- Never leave your devices unattended where someone can access them.
- Don't click on suspicious links.


### Why is it important to lock your computer when away from your desk?
It can allow unauthorized access to your files by anyone. It's especially bad if those files are work-related and contain sensitive information.

### How should you handle phishing attempts and suspicious links?
- Don't click on any links until you are absolutely sure they lead to legitimate sites. If you are still unsure, better to be safe than sorry and not click it at all.
- Always make sure the person on the other line are who they say they are. Double-check their identity.


### What makes a strong password, and why should you use a password manager?
- A strong password is long and contains a combination of letters, numbers, and symbols arranged in a complex and hard to predict manner.
- A password manager stores your passwords securely under a master password and allows you to autofill passwords fields, making it so that you only really need to remember one password and not have to reuse passwords due to the inconvenience of having to remember multiple ones.

### Why is two-factor authentication (2FA) important, and when should you enable it?
It adds an extra security layer by requiring another step when logging in, typically a code with an expiration sent to your phone/email. You should always enable it when you can, whether it be on personal or work accounts, as even if a malicious actor has your login details, as long as 2FA is enabled, they would still need access to your phone or your email account in order to login.

## Reflection

### What security measures do you currently follow, and where can you improve?
I have two-factor authentication enabled on a few of my accounts. A majority of my passwords are also generated by strong password generators, and I change them every few months. As for where I can improve, I can start by enabling two-factor authentication on a lot more of my accounts.

### How can you make secure behaviour a habit rather than an afterthought?
With every account creation, I make sure to use my password manager to store login credentials and generate passwords and enable two-factor authentication as soon as I can. I also dedicate days where I go through my account and change passwords. I also make sure to check if any link actually leads to a legit site before clicking on them.

### What steps will you take to ensure your passwords and accounts are secure?
- Generate long and complex passwords. Use password tester sites to determine the strength of my passwords.
- Use a password manager. Take some time to change passwords on all my accounts every once in a while.
- Enable two-factor authentication wherever possible.
- Never share my login details with anyone.
- Don't fall for phishing scams. Don't click on suspicious links.

### What would you do if you suspected a security breach or suspicious activity on your account?
Change my password and, if it allows me to, log out on all devices. I would also check my devices for evidence of malware that could have caused the incident.

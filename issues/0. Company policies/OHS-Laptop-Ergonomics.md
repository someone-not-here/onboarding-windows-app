## Research and Learn

### What are the risks of using a laptop without an external monitor or keyboard?
- Neck and back pain from slouching.
- Wrist pain (Carpal Tunnel Syndrome) from poor angles and posture.

### What ergonomic equipment can improve posture when working on a laptop?
- Laptop stand. Raises the laptop higher up to eye level.
- External monitor. Reduces neck pain through minimizing head movement.
- Mouse. Can reduce strain on the wrist.
- External keyboard. Keeps wrists and arms in a neutral position.

### What adjustments should be made to monitor height, chair position, and desk setup for a healthier workspace?
- Monitor: raised to eye level, at a balanced distance to where content is still visually clear but does not cause eye strain.  
- Chair: Raised to a height where arms are in a neutral position, back straight, feet on the floor.
- Desk: Keyboard and mouse are at the same level.

### What are some daily habits that reduce the impact of prolonged laptop use?
- Taking breaks to move about and prevent eye strain.
- Maintaining a good posture.
- Adjusting screen brightness and applying a blue light filter.

## Reflection

### What equipment changes can you make to improve your workspace setup?
- Switch to an office chair.
- Get a bigger desk.

### What behavioural changes can you implement to improve posture and reduce strain?
- Sit up straight; don't slouch.
- Stand up and take breaks when needed.
- Keep arms in a neutral position.

### How can you remind yourself to maintain good posture and take breaks throughout the day?
- Use an app, like Focus Bear.
- Form a habit by dedicating times within the day to take a break.

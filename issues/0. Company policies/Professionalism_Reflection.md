## Research and Learn
*What are **examples of professional vs. unprofessional behaviour** in a casual work environment?*

Examples of professional behaviors:
- Delivering work by agreed deadlines, and if there is a delay, communicating with your team as early as possible.
- Responding to messages in a timely manner during work hours (within a few hours or same day).
- Keeping personal drama out of team channels.

Examples of unprofessional behaviors:
- Repeatedly missing deadlines and not offering explanations as to why.
- Ignoring messages or not at least offering any acknowledgement of received messages.
- Taking credit for another teammate's work.

*What does **respectful communication** look like in remote teams?*
Clear, timely, and kind messages that take into consideration time zones and workloads, uses inclusive language, and provides constructive feedback without personal attacks.

*How can you **give and receive feedback professionally**?*

For giving feedback: Be clear, specific, and constructive in your criticisms. Don't just say "this is wrong, fix it", point out the problem and suggest solutions and other ways of tackling the problem. Also, don't make your criticisms personal; focus on the behavior and outcome, not the person.

For receiving feedback: Listen actively without interrupting or defending. If you are unsure of what the other person suggested, ask clarifying questions if needed. Remember to thank the person, and reflect on how to apply it constructively.


## Reflection
*Have you ever experienced or witnessed **great teamwork and professionalism**? What made it effective?*

I've seen some great examples of good teamwork and professionalism in some of the group projects I've been in. The teams I was in that I consider great had a clear assignments and roles, regular check-ins and updates by both the leader and other teammates, and teammates offering advice when one of their own struggles with their task.

*What steps can you take to **make sure your communication is clear, respectful, and inclusive**?*
- Be clear and specific in my messages. Don't be vague.
- Always use respectful language.
- Ask for input from everyone and reflect on their takes.

*How can you help **create a positive, fun, and professional work environment**?*
- Encourage others when they're doing a good job.
- Keep communication friendly but respectful.
- Offer help to a teammate when they need it/ask for it.

*How can you prepare effectively for meetings with supervisors and colleagues?*
- Review the purpose of the meeting, the context surrounding it and the meeting's agenda.
- Pre-write any questions down I might have.
- Be ready to take down notes, especially for the very important points.

*What would it look like to be proactive during your internship?*
- Letting my supervisor know when I've finished a task early.
- Asking clarifying questions when necessary.
- Constantly sharing updates with my team/supervisor regarding my work.

*How might you follow up with colleagues or supervisors if you need information/action from them? How would you escalate it further if you don't get a response and when would you escalate?*
I'll kindly ask them through the appropriate channels and during reasonable hours. If I don't get a response in a long enough time (like a day), I'll send quick and concise follow-up messages over an appropriate interval.

*What are some things you won't do?*
- Spam messages at someone.
- Be inappropriate or disrespectful when communicating with someone.
- Pin my mistakes on others.
- Deliberately ignore someone's messages or feedback.
